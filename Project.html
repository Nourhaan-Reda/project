Knitwear

  • Knitting is a method of interlacing threads and turning them into fabric. A knitted fabric consists of a number of rows of successive loops called stitches, and as the row progresses during knitting, a new loop is pulled through the old loops. The active stitches are held by the needles until the next stitch can pass through, and this process eventually produces the fabric often used for blankets and clothing.


  • Knitting is done by hand or by machine, and there are many methods and methods in hand knitting. Different types of thread and needles can be used to achieve a versatility in knit materials, and these tools give the finished piece a different color, surface, weight and durability. Other factors that affect the final result are needle shape, thickness, ductility, filament composition, filament amplification and bromination.

Beads

  • A bead is a small decorative piece made of glass, plastic, or wood of various types and sizes, perforated and strung together with thick thread. The diameters of the beads range from less than 1 mm to over 1 cm. The oldest known ornament is a pair of beads made from the shells of a sea snail (Nasiris) dating back 100,000 years. Bead making is the art or craft of stringing beads together. The beads are held together with a special thread or flexible wire, or attached to clothing or clay.

Types of beads

  • Beads are divided into several types depending on several criteria, including the components from which the beads are made, the process used in production, the place or time of the bead material, the decoration on the surface, or the general shape.
